\# AAI Demo Script



\## Project Name



Adaptive Action Intelligence (AAI)



\## One-Line Pitch



AAI turns early warning information into explainable, coordinated early action for governments, humanitarian partners, and communities.



\---



\## 1. Opening



Climate early warning is not enough if it does not lead to action.



In Somaliland and Somalia, farmers, pastoralists, local authorities, and humanitarian partners often face rising drought risk, but the information is not always translated into clear decisions.



AAI was built to close that gap.



It answers six operational questions:



1\. What is happening?

2\. Why is it happening?

3\. How serious is it?

4\. What should be done?

5\. Who should act?

6\. What outcome do we expect?



\---



\## 2. Dashboard Entry



The dashboard starts with Mission Control.



Here we can define a climate-related incident such as drought, select the district, and view the weather and location context.



This gives the user a quick situation-awareness layer before moving into the deeper intelligence system.



\---



\## 3. ARIE Intelligence Console



The core engine is ARIE: Adaptive Risk Intelligence Engine.



ARIE converts climate stress, livelihood exposure, coping pressure, water stress, pasture stress, market sensitivity, and coordination readiness into a risk score.



For the Gabiley Drought Watch scenario, ARIE identifies a High Risk situation and recommends enhanced early action coordination.



This is not a black-box score. Every score is linked to drivers, evidence, and a recommended decision.



\---



\## 4. Climate Fusion Trace



The Climate Fusion Trace explains why the risk score changed.



For example, in a dryland farming and agro-pastoral livelihood zone, rainfall deficit and water stress carry higher operational meaning because they affect planting, livestock water access, pasture, and household coping options.



The system shows:



\- Which inputs were adjusted

\- Why they were adjusted

\- What evidence was used

\- What still needs field verification

\- Which communication channels should be used



This makes the system explainable and useful for coordination.



\---



\## 5. AIDA Decision Partner



AIDA is the communication layer.



It does not calculate risk. ARIE calculates risk.



AIDA translates ARIE intelligence into different formats for:



\- Senior decision-makers

\- Government and DRM actors

\- Humanitarian partners

\- Communities

\- Somali-speaking last-mile users



This matters because the same warning must be understandable to different audiences.



\---



\## 6. Operational Products



The Operational Products panel turns intelligence into ready-to-use outputs.



It generates:



\- Situation Report

\- Government Action Note

\- Humanitarian Coordination Note

\- Community Advisory

\- Somali Last-Mile Message



These products can be copied and used in coordination meetings, community radio, WhatsApp groups, field validation, and early action planning.



\---



\## 7. Why AAI Is Different



AAI is not just a dashboard.



It connects climate risk with livelihood realities, field verification, coordination logic, and last-mile communication.



It moves from:



Warning issued



to:



Decision made



to:



Action assigned



to:



Message delivered



to:



Outcome expected



\---



\## 8. Somaliland / Somalia Relevance



AAI is designed for dryland and agro-pastoral contexts where drought, water stress, pasture pressure, and delayed action can quickly affect livelihoods.



The Gabiley scenario reflects smallholder farming and agro-pastoral systems.



The Togdheer scenario reflects mobile pastoral livelihoods, water-point pressure, pasture stress, and livestock movement decisions.



This makes the platform locally grounded while still scalable across the IGAD region.



\---

The next version can integrate:

\- CHIRPS rainfall data

\- MODIS or Sentinel vegetation indicators

\- WorldPop exposure layers

\- Market price monitoring

\- WhatsApp and SMS dissemination

\- Field validation feedback loops



\---



\## 10. Closing



AAI helps decision-makers act before households lose options.



The goal is not only to predict risk.



The goal is to make climate intelligence actionable, explainable, and usable from the coordination room to the community level.
